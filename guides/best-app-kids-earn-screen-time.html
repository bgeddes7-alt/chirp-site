What to look for in any earn screen time app

Verification before earning. The best apps require proof before minutes are awarded — a photo or parent confirmation. Without verification, kids learn to claim tasks without doing them.

Accumulated balance, not daily reset. If earned minutes expire at midnight, kids lose motivation to save and the system feels punitive. A bank balance that carries over teaches planning and self-regulation.

Device enforcement, not just tracking. An app that tracks time but doesn't enforce it at the device level still requires you to manually cut off access — which is where arguments start. The best apps block or unlock at the device level automatically.

Gaming console support. Most parental control apps handle phones and tablets but have no path to gaming consoles. If your family has a Nintendo Switch (the most common gaming console for kids ages 6-12), look specifically for Switch integration.
