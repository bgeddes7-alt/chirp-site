Common questions

Answers, before you ask.

Everything parents typically want to know about Chirp.

What is Chirp?

Chirp is a family task and reward app where kids earn screen time and other rewards by completing parent-assigned tasks. Parents create tasks with assigned reward minutes, kids submit completed tasks with photo proof, parents approve, and earned time can be redeemed across supported platforms — including automatic Nintendo Switch enforcement.

How does Chirp work?

Parents set up tasks (chores, homework, reading, anything) with screen-time minute rewards. Kids see their tasks on their own screen, complete them, and submit with a photo. Parents get a push notification, approve in one tap, and the earned minutes go into the child's balance. Kids start a timer to use their earned time, and on Nintendo Switch the daily play limit is raised and reset automatically.

How does Nintendo Switch enforcement work?

Chirp connects directly to Nintendo's Parental Controls API. When a child starts a Chirp timer, their Switch daily play limit is increased by the earned amount. When the timer ends, the limit resets automatically. No nagging, no honor system.

What ages is Chirp designed for?

Chirp is built for families with kids roughly ages 5 to 14. Younger kids handle simple tasks with parent help, older kids manage their own task lists and balances. The kid-facing screens are designed to be readable and motivating for early elementary readers and up.

Is Chirp free?

Chirp has a free tier that includes 1 child, 5 active tasks, manual task approval, and basic balance tracking. The Family plan unlocks up to 5 children, unlimited tasks, Nintendo Switch integration, push notifications, leaderboards, streaks, sibling gifting, and trends.

What devices does Chirp support?

Chirp is launching on iOS for iPhone and iPad (iOS 16+). Android support is planned. Nintendo Switch enforcement works regardless of which phone the parent uses, since it talks directly to Nintendo's parental controls.

When will Chirp be available?

Chirp is in active TestFlight beta and preparing for App Store launch. Once the listing is live, this site will link directly to it.

How is my family's data protected?

Chirp does not sell or share family data with advertisers. Photo submissions are stored in row-level-security-protected storage so only your family can access them. Full details are in our Privacy Policy.
